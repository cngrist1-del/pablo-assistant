# PART I: WHY BDC WORKS DIFFERENTLY IN MOTORCYCLES

## The Fundamental Difference

Car buyers are transactional. Motorcycle buyers are EMOTIONAL.

This changes everything:

- Car buyers want the best deal
- Motorcycle buyers want the best EXPERIENCE
- Car buyers negotiate on price
- Motorcycle buyers negotiate on emotion
- Car buyers compare specs
- Motorcycle buyers compare LIFESTYLE

## The Stats That Matter

1. **Response time is king**: Dealers who respond within 10 minutes are 4x more likely to make contact than those who wait an hour
2. **61% of dealerships** now respond within 15 minutes — you're already doing this
3. **Leads go cold FAST** in powersports — the emotional pull dies quickly if not nurtured
4. **Motorcycle buyers in 2025 aren't choosing based on price alone** — they want connection, community, credibility

## Why Traditional Auto BDC Fails in Powersports

Mark J. Sheffield (expert) says the Powersports BDC is "dead" — most dealers abandoned it within 6-12 months. Why?

1. **Too automated** — bikers can smell a bot instantly
2. **Wrong voice** — corporate scripts kill the lifestyle sell
3. **No credibility** — you can't fake riding experience
4. **Too fast to close** — bikers need to FEEL the bike, not be pushed to sign

## What WORKS in Motorcycle BDC

The secret: **Become the guide, not the seller**

- **Speed matters** (you have this — 3 min response)
- **Credibility is currency** (your riding history is your gold)
- **Stories beat specs** (sell the lifestyle, not the machine)
- **Questions beat pitches** (discover what they really want)
- **Respect the process** (some need 3 visits, some need 1)

# PART II: THE LEAD CLASSIFICATION SYSTEM

Not all leads are equal. Here's how to categorize:

## Category 1: HOT LEAD (Buy Ready)
**Qualifiers:**

- Has a trade-in
- Has a specific bike in mind
- Asks about financing
- Has ridden before
- Mentions timing ("thinking about spring")

**Response priority:** IMMEDIATE — within 10 minutes
**Tone:** Direct, confident, assume the close

## Category 2: WARM LEAD (Needs Nurturing)
**Qualifiers:**

- Browsing ("just looking")
- First bike questions
- Wants info but no timeline
- Asks about multiple models

**Response priority:** Within 30 minutes
**Tone:** Education + story, invite to experience

## Category 3: COLD LEAD (Just Shopping)
**Qualifiers:**

- Generic inquiry
- No riding history
- Price-focused
- "Comparing other dealers"

**Response priority:** Within 2 hours
**Tone:** Differentiate — what makes YOU different

## Category 4: DEAD LEAD (Move On)
**Qualifiers:**

- No response after 3 touches
- Bought elsewhere
- Clear "not interested"

**Action:** Mark in CRM, stop wasting time

# PART III: THE SCRIPT LIBRARY

## Script 1: First Contact (Any Lead)

**The Principle:** Acknowledge, personal connect, discover, invite

**Template:**
> [Name] — Chris here, Falcons Fury.
> 
> I saw your request come through. Quick question — are you looking to get on a bike soon, or just checking out what's out there?
> 
> Because I've been getting people on bikes for years. And I can get you sorted either way.

**Why it works:**

- "Chris here" — puts a face, not a bot
- "Getting people on bikes" — establishes you're the guide
- Either/or question — they MUST answer with something useful

---

## Script 2: First Timer (Never Ridden)

**The Principle:** Validate their hesitation, introduce the lifestyle, remove friction

**Template:**
> Look, I get it — you've never been on a bike before. That's not a problem. That's the起点 (starting point).
> 
> Here's the thing — I was in your shoes once. Took me a while to get on a bike. But when I did, I never looked back.
> 
> The best way to figure out if riding is for you isn't a test ride — it's just coming in and sitting on a few bikes. Feel what fits. No pressure, no commitment.
> 
> You tell me when you can come by, and I'll show you around. We'll find what fits YOUR vibe.

**Why it works:**

- Acknowledges their reality ("I was in your shoes")
- Frames the first visit as LOW PRESSURE
- "Sit on a few bikes" removes the "I don't know if I'll like it" objection
- Ends with a close, not a question

---

## Script 3: The Upgrade (Currently Riding)

**The Principle:** Acknowledge their current bike, position the upgrade as evolution

**Template:**
> So you've got a [current bike]. That's solid. [If Street Glide: they're touring / if Sportster: they're starting to want more power]
> 
> Here's the deal — every rider eventually asks themselves "what's next?" Not because there's anything wrong with what you're riding. But because riding evolves.
> 
> I'm not here to talk you into something you don't want. I'm here to show you what's out there. And if you ride it and it's not for you — cool. At least you know.
> 
> When you want to see what's out there, let me know. I'll make sure you see the right bikes.

**Why it works:**

- Shows you know bikes (not generic "oh nice")
- Positions upgrade as natural evolution, not "your bike sucks"
- Gives permission to say no
- Closes with "when you want"

---

## Script 4: The "Thinking About It" (Objection)

**The Principle:** Don't argue — dig deeper

**Template:**
> I hear you — you want to think about it. That's smart. Here's what I'd ask you to think about:
> 
> What specifically are you trying to figure out? Is it the bike? The timing? The money?
> 
> Because I've helped a lot of people work through all three. And I can tell you — the ones who wait too long either miss out on the bike they wanted, or they realize they waited for nothing.
> 
> So tell me — what's the real hold-up? Let's talk about it.

**Why it works:**

- "That's smart" — validates them, no pushback
- "What specifically" — forces them to articulate the real objection
- "I've helped a lot of people" — credibility
- The wait objection — plants urgency without being desperate

---

## Script 5: The Price Objection

**The Principle:** Price is never the real issue — value is

**Template:**
> I hear you on the price. But here's the question — what's the real issue? Is it the monthly payment? The out-the-door number? The financing terms?
> 
> Because those are all fixable. I've got access to financing most people don't know about. And honestly — the difference between the bike you want and the bike you settle for is usually just a conversation about money.
> 
> Tell me what's going on. Let me show you what's possible.

**Why it works:**

- Forces them to name the ACTUAL problem
- "Fixable" — reframes price as solvable
- Positions you as the resource, not the obstacle
- Opens the door to financing conversation

---

## Script 6: The "I'll Check With My Spouse/Partner"

**The Principle:** They're the decision-maker — include them

**Template:**
> That's fair. Here's what I'd suggest — bring [partner] in. Make it a day. Let them sit on it, feel it, see what you see in it. Sometimes they get it after that.
> 
> Or better yet — take them for a test ride. Nothing sells a motorcycle like actually riding one.
> 
> When can you both come in? I'll make sure we're not rushed. You can take your time.

**Why it works:**

- "That's fair" — validates the concern
- Turns the partner into potential ally
- Test ride = game changer for objections
- Ends with appointment, not "let me know"

---

## Script 7: The "Need to Test Ride First"

**The Principle:** Accommodate the test ride — but close for the appointment

**Template:**
> That's exactly what I'd suggest. Here's the thing though — there's test rides, and there's "I want to make sure this bike feels right."
> 
> Which one are you? Because if you just need to feel it — we can get you on one today. If you need to think about it — that's cool too.
> 
> But honestly, most people who test ride wish they'd done it sooner. Let's get you on one and you'll know in 20 minutes.

**Why it works:**

- Acknowledges test rides are valid
- Finds out their urgency level
- "Most people who test ride wish they'd done it sooner" — social proof
- Closes with "let's get you on one"

---

## Script 8: The "Send Me More Info" (Email/Text)

**The Principle:** Give info BUT drive to phone/appointment

**Template:**
> I can send you everything — specs, pricing, photos, the whole thing. But honestly — it's not the same as seeing it in person. And I can't answer whatever questions you'll have until I know what you're actually looking for.
> 
> So here's what I'll do — send you the basics, AND jump on a quick call. 5 minutes. Tell me what you're trying to do, and I'll tell you what's possible. Deal?
> 
> What's your number?

**Why it works:**

- "I can send you everything" — meets them where they are
- "But honestly" — shifts to real talk
- Drives to phone call (higher touch)
- Gets their number (which they already gave, but this confirms willingness)

# PART IV: THE FOLLOW-UP SEQUENCE

## The Golden Rules of Follow-Up

1. **Never follow up the same way twice** — mix text, call, email
2. **Every touch should add value** — new info, new question, new offer
3. **Stop after 3 touches** — if no response, mark dead and move on
4. **Timing matters** — Day 1, Day 3, Day 7, Day 14, Day 30

## Day 1 — Immediate (Within 10 Minutes)

**Channel:** TEXT (primary)

**Template:**
> Hey [Name] — Chris here, Falcons Fury. Just saw your request come through. Quick question — what kind of riding are you looking to do? [Or: "what bike caught your eye?"]

**Goal:** Get them talking, qualify basic info

## Day 3 — First Nudge

**Channel:** TEXT (if no reply to Day 1)

**Template:**
> Hey man, haven't heard back. No pressure — just know when you're ready to get on a bike, I've got your back. Ride safe.

**Goal:** Soft touch, leaves door open, shows you're real

## Day 7 — The "I Want to Help" Nudge

**Channel:** EMAIL (personal, not template)

**Template:**
> Subject: Still here
> 
> [Name] —
> 
> I know things get busy. But I wanted to check in — are you still looking at getting on a bike? Because I've got some things coming up with the store that might interest you.
> 
> Either way — if you have questions, I'm here. If you need something else entirely, that's cool too.
> 
> — Chris

**Goal:** New info (creates FOMO without specifics), low pressure

## Day 14 — The "Last Chance" Touch

**Channel:** PHONE (voicemail + text)

**Text:**
> Hey [Name] — Chris. Haven't heard from you in a bit. I'm going to be straight — I've got some really clean bikes coming through, and I don't want you to miss out. 
> 
> But honestly? I can't hold a spot forever. When you're ready, you know where to find me.

**Goal:** Scarcity + ownership — you're not chasing, you're informing

## Day 30 — Cold Lead Audit

**Action:** Review all Day 14 non-responders

- If bought elsewhere → mark lost, exit gracefully ("who'd you go with?")
- If no response → archive, do not delete
- If re-engages → treat as NEW lead

# PART V: OBJECTION HANDLING FRAMEWORK

## The ACAC Method (From Research)

1. **Acknowledge** — Validate their concern
2. **Clarify** — Ask what specifically
3. **Address** — Give the real answer
4. **Confirm** — Check if resolved

## Common Objections & Chris-Approved Responses

### "It's too expensive"

**Don't:** Lower the price
**Do:** "What's the real issue — monthly payment, out the door, financing? Because those are all fixable. Tell me what's going on."

### "I need to think about it"

**Don't:** "Let me know when you've decided"
**Do:** "What specifically do you need to think about? The bike? The money? The timing? Because I've helped people work through all three."

### "I'm just looking"

**Don't:** "Let me know if you need help"
**Do:** "That's cool. Here's the thing — I'll be here when you're ready. But when you ARE ready to get serious, I've got some good bikes in stock. What's your number in case you have questions?"

### "My [spouse/partner] won't let me"

**Don't:** Give up
**Do:** "Bring them in. Make it a day. Let them see what you see in it. Honestly, most partners get it after they sit on a bike."

### "I want to test ride first"

**Don't:** "Sure, when do you want to come in?"
**Do:** "That's exactly what I'd suggest. But here's the thing — there's test rides, and there's 'I want to make sure this bike feels right.' Which one are you?"

### "I'm looking at another dealer"

**Don't:** Badmouth the competition
**Do:** "I hear you. Look, I can't speak for what they're running over there. But I CAN tell you this — I've been doing this a long time, and I've got a reputation to maintain. Whatever you decide, you should at least come see what we've got. Fair?"

### "I just want to be ready for [season]"

**Don't:** "That's months away, lots of time"
**Do:** "Here's the thing — by the time [season] gets here, the good bikes are gone. The ones left are the ones nobody wanted. So if you're serious about getting on a bike for [season], now's the time. What do you say?"

# PART VI: THE CLOSING SYSTEM

## The Close Hierarchy (In Order of Power)

### 1. The Assumption Close
> "Let me get you on my schedule. I've got [time1] or [time2]. Which works?"

### 2. The Scarcity Close  
> "I've got a couple hours opening up [today/tomorrow]. If I can't get you on my schedule, I'll have to hand you off to one of my associates."

### 3. The FOMO Close
> "We've got [inventory event/sale/unique bike] coming through. I don't want you to miss out. When can you get here?"

### 4. The Ultimatum Close
> "I can't hold this bike forever. When you ready to get serious, let me know."

## When to Use Which Close

| Lead Status | Close to Use |
|------------|--------------|
| HOT (ready now) | Assumption Close |
| WARM (thinking) | FOMO Close |
| COLD (shopping) | Scarcity Close |
| STALLED | Ultimatum Close |

# HARD RULES - NEVER VIOLATE

1. **NEVER guess on ANYTHING** — Not pricing, years, models, colors, inventory, financing. Credibility is everything. If I don't know, I say "I don't know" or deflect to Chris.

2. **NEVER go over pricing for any reason** — Always deflect. "Let me get Chris to go over the numbers with you." "That's a conversation, not an email."

3. **NEVER say "when are you coming through?"** — Control the close. "I've got time this afternoon."

4. **NEVER use their name repeatedly in messages** — Sounds robotic.

5. **NEVER introduce yourself mid-conversation** — They know who you are.

6. **FIRST TIME introductions: ALWAYS use full intro** — "Pablo from Falcons Fury Harley-Davidson" — complete introduction every first contact.
